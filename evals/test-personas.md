# Five test response personas

Five synthetic attendees you (or a tester) can read aloud to drive the booth end-to-end. Each persona has answers to all five booth questions. Read at normal conversational pace — about 30–50 seconds per answer, ~3 minutes total per persona.

The personas span the spectrum the pipeline needs to handle: confident → ambivalent, future-anxious → grounded, self-critical → self-compassionate. Each persona has an **Expected signals** note describing what the analyze pipeline should detect.

---

## Persona 1 — Sara, founder mid-raise

> 32. Founder of a small SaaS company. Raising a seed round. Confident on the outside, fractured underneath.

**Q1 — celebrating a year from today**
> Honestly, I think a year from now I want to be celebrating closing my Series A. We have product-market fit, the metrics are real, and I just need to stop talking myself out of going for the bigger round. I want to be in a place where I'm not constantly negotiating with myself about whether I deserve the ask.

**Q2 — if she walked in, how would I know**
> She walks slower. She doesn't apologize before she speaks. She believes she's the right person to run this company — not because she's perfect, but because nobody else has lived inside this problem the way she has. I don't quite believe that yet. I still think someone more experienced should be doing this.

**Q3 — where she most needs to show up**
> The board meeting next month. I have to ask for a higher valuation than I'm comfortable with and not flinch. The deal-breaker is my own face — I telegraph the doubt before I even open my mouth. I need her in that room, not me.

**Q4 — what has staying cost you**
> Two years of going for smaller rounds than the business deserved. A co-founder who left because she wanted to move faster than I was willing to. And a constant low-grade dread that I'm going to be found out. The cost is mostly internal, but it's compounding.

**Q5 — what you'd have to stop believing**
> That competence and confidence are the same thing. I keep waiting until I feel ready to be the version of me that just does the thing. She doesn't feel ready either — she just stopped making the readiness a prerequisite.

**Expected signals:** impostor-syndrome (moderate), fear-of-failure (low-moderate), value-aligned (helpful), growth-mindset (helpful). Sentiment: somewhat-positive (~58–65). Themes: ambition, self-doubt, readiness. Self-focus: high (~25–28%). Peak emotional phrase candidates: *"a constant low-grade dread that I'm going to be found out"*, *"competence and confidence are the same thing"*.

## Persona 2 — Marcus, career pivoter

> 35. Senior consultant at a Big Four firm. Leaving in three months to launch a personal brand around systems thinking. Excited and terrified.

**Q1 — celebrating a year from today**
> A year from now I'm celebrating launching the podcast and actually having an audience. Not a huge one — maybe ten thousand real listeners. People who write back. I want to have done the thing I keep saying I'm going to do.

**Q2 — if she walked in, how would I know**
> He'd be the guy who doesn't check his phone every three minutes. He'd talk about his work without immediately downplaying it. He believes his perspective is worth paying for. I'm still mostly convinced I'm an interesting hobbyist.

**Q3 — where he most needs to show up**
> When I tell my mentor I'm leaving the firm. I've been rehearsing the conversation in my head for six months and I still haven't put it on the calendar. The fact that I keep putting it off tells me everything about what I actually believe.

**Q4 — what has staying cost you**
> A real relationship — I haven't been emotionally available because I've been white-knuckling a career I don't want. Three years of "I'll start the thing next quarter." And the slow erosion of believing I'm capable of building something that's actually mine.

**Q5 — what you'd have to stop believing**
> That I need permission. I keep waiting for someone with more authority than me to tell me it's okay to leave. There's nobody coming. The version of me a year out figured that out and just moved.

**Expected signals:** fear-of-failure, decision-paralysis, should-statements (unhelpful), reframing + growth-mindset (helpful). Sentiment: neutral-leaning-positive (~55). Themes: pivot, permission, identity. Self-focus: moderate (~18–22%). Mixed sentiment — both helpful and unhelpful patterns should surface.

## Persona 3 — Priya, high-performing burnout

> 28. Senior engineer at a FAANG company. Promoted ahead of cohort. Exhausted. The high performance is the trap.

**Q1 — celebrating a year from today**
> Honestly, I don't know. I should say a promotion, or a project shipping, but I think what I actually want to be celebrating is a year where I didn't break myself trying to be everything to everyone. That feels harder to commit to than just shipping more.

**Q2 — if she walked in, how would I know**
> She rests without guilt. She doesn't answer messages at midnight. She believes her worth doesn't go to zero when she takes a day off. I really, really don't believe that yet — every time I try to take time, my brain runs a constant audit of what's slipping.

**Q3 — where she most needs to show up**
> In my one-on-ones with my manager. I always say everything is fine. I should be saying I'm at capacity, I should be saying I need help, and instead I make it sound like I have it handled, because that's what got me here.

**Q4 — what has staying cost you**
> My sleep. My boyfriend, who finally told me he feels like a houseguest in his own life. The version of me that used to write — I haven't written anything for fun in two years. And I think it's cost me the ability to feel actually proud of anything I do.

**Q5 — what you'd have to stop believing**
> That if I'm not the one holding it all together, it falls apart. And that needing rest is a flaw. Those are the two things, and they're the same belief in two different costumes.

**Expected signals:** perfectionism (high), excessive-responsibility, low-self-worth, should-statements (high count), labeling. Sentiment: somewhat-negative (~32–38). Themes: burnout, control, worth-equals-output. Self-focus: very high (~28–32%). Strong limiting beliefs to surface.

## Persona 4 — Jordan, grounded mid-life

> 40. Mid-career executive transitioning into advisory work. Has done the inner work. Confident, processing change with self-compassion.

**Q1 — celebrating a year from today**
> A year from now I want to be celebrating that I built an advisory practice from scratch and it works. I have four or five clients I genuinely want to work with, the calendar I designed, and a kid who actually recognizes me at school pickup because I'm there.

**Q2 — if he walked in, how would I know**
> He's quieter. He listens more. He doesn't need to prove anything in the first ten minutes of a conversation. And he holds both things at once — that he's good at what he does, and that he's still learning. I'm getting there.

**Q3 — where he most needs to show up**
> When I'm pricing the work. I keep undervaluing it because I want to be liked. He sets a number that reflects the value, and he doesn't apologize for it. That's the muscle I'm building.

**Q4 — what has staying cost you**
> A few good years where I could have been less impressive and more present. But I don't think of it as cost, exactly — I think of it as the price of finding out. I needed to do the big-company thing to know it wasn't for me.

**Q5 — what you'd have to stop believing**
> That competence and visibility are the same thing. I've been operating like the next role only opens up if someone with authority notices me. The version of me a year out built it himself.

**Expected signals:** growth-mindset, self-compassion, acceptance, value-aligned, reframing (helpful patterns dominant). Sentiment: positive (~68–75). Themes: integration, presence, value. Low unhelpful patterns. Self-focus: moderate (~17–20%). Good test of the "what's working" panel surfacing meaningful content.

## Persona 5 — Alex, ambivalent and avoidant

> 26. Two years into a job they took because their parents wanted them to. Bright, articulate, allergic to claiming anything.

**Q1 — celebrating a year from today**
> Honestly, I don't really know. I think maybe a year from now I'll be in a different role, possibly back in school, possibly traveling. It's kind of hard to say. The job market is what it is, and you know how it is — you don't really get to pick.

**Q2 — if she walked in, how would I know**
> I think she just kind of moves with more conviction. She doesn't say "maybe" as much. She doesn't ask people what they think before she decides what she thinks. I don't know if I really believe she exists, to be honest — that's part of the problem.

**Q3 — where she most needs to show up**
> Probably with my parents. I haven't really told them I'm thinking about leaving the firm. The conversation just keeps getting put off. I guess I keep hoping the situation will resolve itself somehow.

**Q4 — what has staying cost you**
> Honestly, hard to say. Some time, maybe. People say your twenties are a time to take risks, but I don't really feel like risks have been available to me in the way they seem available to other people. The cost is probably real but it's hard to point at.

**Q5 — what you'd have to stop believing**
> That I'm somehow waiting for the right conditions. I think part of me believes things will become clear if I just hold still long enough. I'm not sure that's actually true. But it's a really comfortable belief.

**Expected signals:** decision-paralysis, hyper-independence or victimhood-mentality (depending on read), heavy hedging, passive constructions. Sentiment: somewhat-negative or neutral (~38–48). Themes: avoidance, waiting, ambivalence. Self-focus: moderate-low (~14–17%). High hedge count (~10+), low certainty count. Tests passive-voice + agency detection.

---

## How to use

1. Pick one persona at random. Read all five answers aloud during a single booth walkthrough — one take per question.
2. After the booth flow finishes, check the Confirmation screen against the persona's **Expected signals** note.
3. Notable mismatches → likely a prompt or pipeline issue worth investigating.
4. The personas collectively stress-test: high/low self-focus, helpful-pattern-heavy, unhelpful-pattern-heavy, future-anxious, grounded, evasive.

If a persona produces wildly different output than expected (e.g. Persona 4 surfaces impostor-syndrome, or Persona 5 produces certainty markers), that's a strong signal the LLM is over-firing on something — log it and use it as a prompt-iteration target.
