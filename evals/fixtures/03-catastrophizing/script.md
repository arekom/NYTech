# 03-catastrophizing

A script designed to exhibit clear catastrophizing + fortune-telling + future-anxiety patterns. Heavy "what if" usage, worst-case spiraling, control-issues language.

**Question being answered:** *"What has staying where you are actually cost you?"*

---

## Read this aloud at normal pace (~45–60 seconds)

> Honestly, I think about it constantly. I keep playing it out. If I don't make a move soon, the whole thing falls apart. I see my friends taking risks and I just freeze. What if I leave the job and the market turns? What if I take the round and we don't hit our numbers and the whole company implodes and I've burned three years on this? My brain just goes to the worst-case every single time. I lie awake at night running through all the ways it could go wrong. The cost is — I've stayed in the same role for two years past when I should have moved. I haven't taken the meeting I need to take. I haven't made the ask. I keep telling myself I'll do it when I'm ready, but I don't know if I'll ever be ready, and meanwhile every door I don't walk through is closing.

---

## Why this script exists

- **Catastrophizing**: "the whole thing falls apart", "the whole company implodes", "burned three years on this", "every door I don't walk through is closing"
- **Fortune-telling**: "what if the market turns", "what if we don't hit our numbers"
- **Future-anxiety** as a limiting belief
- **Control-issues** (decision-paralysis underneath): "I'll do it when I'm ready" repeated
- **Rumination**: "I think about it constantly", "I lie awake at night running through all the ways"
- **Should-statement**: "past when I should have moved"
- Sentiment: clearly negative
- Dominant emotion: fear, anxiety (the *concept* — the forbidden-word guard catches the word "anxiety" appearing in OUTPUT, the speaker can say whatever)
- Self-focus: very high
- Verbatim quote candidates: "running through all the ways it could go wrong", "every door I don't walk through is closing", "the whole thing falls apart"
