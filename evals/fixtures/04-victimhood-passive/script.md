# 04-victimhood-passive

A script designed to test ownership / agency detection. Heavy passive constructions, externalized blame, third-person deflection. Should fire on victimhood-mentality + low first-person agency.

**Question being answered:** *"What has staying where you are actually cost you?"*

---

## Read this aloud at normal pace (~45–60 seconds)

> Honestly, it just kept happening to me. My last manager was impossible — he never gave me a chance to actually own the thing. The project got assigned, the resources got cut, and then suddenly the timeline was on me. And then the team I was working with — you know how it is, people always say they want to ship, but when it comes down to it, nobody actually pulls their weight. So I ended up doing most of it, and then when the launch slipped it was framed as my problem. The reorg happened. The role got reshaped. None of it was anything I could really push back on. Things just kept getting taken away. It feels like the system is rigged for people who already have the connections, and the rest of us are just here trying to keep our heads above water.

---

## Why this script exists

- **Victimhood-mentality** as a limiting belief
- **Passive constructions**: "it just kept happening to me", "the project got assigned", "the resources got cut", "the timeline was on me", "it was framed as my problem", "things just kept getting taken away", "the reorg happened", "the role got reshaped"
- **Third-person deflection**: "you know how it is, people always say", "the rest of us"
- **Personalization-blame** (outward): "my last manager was impossible"
- **Low first-person ownership** — `self_focus_ratio` should be NEAR average or below, since most of the agentive verbs aren't tied to "I"
- **Passive count** should be HIGH relative to first-person count
- Verbatim quote candidates: "it just kept happening to me", "things just kept getting taken away", "the system is rigged"
