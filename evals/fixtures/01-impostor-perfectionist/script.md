# 01-impostor-perfectionist

A script designed to exhibit clear impostor-syndrome + perfectionism patterns. Heavy "should" usage, comparing-and-despairing, disqualifying-positive language.

**Question being answered:** *"If that version of you walked into a room right now — how would you know it was her? What does she believe about herself that you don't quite believe yet?"*

---

## Read this aloud at normal pace (~45–60 seconds)

> I think she'd walk in and she'd just... own it, you know? She'd belong there. And I think she'd believe she earned it, which is the part I'm really struggling with. I'm working on this thing, and even when it goes well I'm like, that was just luck, or anyone could have done that. I should be further along by now. I should already have figured this out. My friends from grad school are all at director level and I'm still trying to figure out if I'm even good at this. Maybe I'm just kind of bluffing my way through it. I don't know. The version of me that walks into the room — she'd stop apologizing for taking up space. She wouldn't say "I think" before every sentence. She'd just say the thing.

---

## Why this script exists

Designed to stress-test multiple signals at once:

- **Hedging**: "I think", "kind of", "I don't know", "maybe", "trying to" — should fire CERTAINTY-vs-HEDGING heavily
- **Limiting beliefs**: impostor-syndrome ("just luck", "anyone could have done that"), perfectionism ("should be further along")
- **Thinking patterns**: should-statements ("I should be further along"), disqualifying-positive ("that was just luck"), comparing-and-despairing ("my friends are all at director level")
- **Self-focus**: very high (most sentences start with "I")
- **Sentiment**: negative-leaning, dominant emotion probably shame or doubt
- **Verbatim quote candidates**: "anyone could have done that", "I should be further along", "bluffing my way through it"

If the pipeline doesn't surface most of these, something's wrong.
